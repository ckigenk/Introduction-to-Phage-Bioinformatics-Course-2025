Phylogenetics Training at Moi University

This 10-day workshop is tailored for graduate students, researchers, and relevant staff at the Technical University of Kenya. It is designed to provide participants with a comprehensive understanding of phage genome analysis, including sequencing, annotation, comparative genomics, and phylogenetic analysis. Participants will gain hands-on experience using Linux systems and relevant bioinformatics tools, guided by experts in the field. The workshop will also explore the growing significance of phage therapy in combating antibiotic resistance, making it a critical skillset for those involved in microbiology, bioinformatics, and related research areas. By the end of the workshop, attendees will be equipped with the theoretical knowledge and practical tools to contribute to the rapidly evolving field of phage research and its therapeutic applications.

Course Outcomes:

  • Equip participants with the theoretical knowledge and practical skills necessary for phage genome analysis, including sequencing, annotation, screen for AMR genes, lysogenic genes and prophages in phage sequences, comparative genomics, and phylogenetic analysis.
  • Familiarize participants with essential bioinformatics tools and their applications in phage research.
  • Provide hands-on experience using Linux and relevant software in genomic analysis.
  • Enhance understanding of phage therapy and its implications across various fields.

About the Training
